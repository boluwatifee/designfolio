Overview

The challenge was to design a mobile app that will allow users to book flights, accommodation, and car for different destinations, while also addressing the user’s pain points.

Goal

What should EasyTravel achieve?

  • Design an easy to use app with a friendly neat interface
  • Make it easy for users to find fun activities at their holiday destinations
  • Ensure user can organize all their travel plans in one itinery

    • Problem

    Booking and plannning trips haven't been an easy one

    Many People find it exhausting having to use multiple apps to: search for interesting places to visit, book flights, check for favourable weather conditions, plan their trips and so on.

    Research

    Primary Research

    Before I began, I wanted to outline the scope of my research. I created a research plan to help keep me focused on the research goals at all times, as well as the steps I needed to take to complete it. I primarily wanted to get answers to some of the most pressing questions I had:

  • What are people's motivations and pain points when it comes to trip planning and management?
  • How can people tell the "best time" to visit a city?
  • What features would they look for when booking a vacation through a travel agency online?
    •

    Target Market

    23-35 year olds

    Key Insights from Research

    Based on the research I carried out, The use of mobile travel apps simplifies vacation planning significantly and most users would love one that simplifies the process even more. They would love for an app that is user friendly and meets all basic needs like booking flight and accomodation.

After the tourism growth of 2022 and 2021, international arrivals will continue to recover by 2022.     Takeaways

    While working on this project, I learned more about User Centered Design as I interviewed target users, I got to understand and empathize with them then ensured that their noted pain points were at the front of my mind while designing. Both the secondary and primary research helped shape my thinking and influenced my design decision process.

  • Worrying less about details. I was initially overly focused on the UI but I quickly realized there is no great product without a properly structured UX.
  • It is important to keep the users’ pain points front and center. This helped to shape my thinking, and made me more focused on solving the problem. I asked myself questions like: How do I want my users to feel? How can I make their lives easier?
  • Based on usability testing, some users did not want to have to google every location; instead, they wanted to be able to get adequte information about different places right from the app, which the app provides. Some others wanted an easier way to keep track of travel deals without constantly checking the app.
